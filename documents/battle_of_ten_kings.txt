Battle of Ten Kings (Rigveda – Daśarājña)
Tags: Rigveda 7.18; related 7.33, 7.83 | Also written: Dasarajna, Parushni, Vasistha, Vishvamitra, Sudasa

ONE-LINE DEFINITIONS (for quick retrieval)
• Bharata (tribe): Vedic Indo-Aryan tribe led by King Sudās; allied with priest Vasiṣṭha; later merges with the Purus → seeds of the Kuru polity; “Bhārata” later becomes a name for India.
• Paruṣṇī (Ravi): River in Punjab commonly identified with the Ravi.
• Daśarājña (“Battle of Ten Kings”): Rigvedic conflict where Sudās defeats a coalition of tribes.

KEY FACTS
• Event: Battle of Ten Kings (Daśarājña) in the Rigveda [primary hymn 7.18; related 7.33, 7.83].
• Victor: King Sudās of the Bharatas.
• Location: Banks of the Paruṣṇī (often equated with the Ravi).
• Timeframe: Early Vedic period (likely 2nd millennium BCE; exact date unknown).
• Religious frame: Hymns credit Indra and proper ritual/leadership for victory.

WHO FOUGHT
• Sudās (Bharata) vs a coalition ≈ ten tribes.
• Common lists include: Puru, Anu, Druhyu, Yadu, Turvaśa, Alina, Bhalāna, Paktha, Śiva, Viśānin (lists vary by hymn/translator).
• Priestly politics: Vasiṣṭha aligned with Sudās; rivalry with Viśvāmitra appears in the background of these alignments.

TACTICS & NARRATIVE NOTES
• River tactic: Sudās uses the crossing to disrupt the coalition’s formation.
• Chariots and divine aid (Indra) are praised for protecting the Bharata host.

WHY IT MATTERED
• Consolidation: Bharata dominance in upper Indus–Sarasvatī–Ravi region.
• Later developments: Bharatas mingle with Purus → seen as a step toward Kuru formation.
• Memory: Early Vedic “origin story” of power shifts among tribes.

UNCERTAINTIES & DEBATES
• Exact date indeterminate; Rigveda is layered/compiled.
• Tribe lists vary by hymn and translation.
• Geography specifics remain debated though Paruṣṇī≈Ravi is common.

MINI GLOSSARY (retrieval-ready)
• Bharata: Vedic tribe of Sudās; later linked to the name “Bhārata” for India; unites with Purus → proto-Kuru.
• Vasiṣṭha / Viśvāmitra (Vasistha / Vishvamitra): Influential seers (ṛṣis) tied to rival factions.
• Paruṣṇī (Parushni): River usually identified with the Ravi in Punjab.
• Daśarājña (Dasarajna): “Battle of Ten Kings”.

ALTERNATE/ASCII SPELLINGS (to improve matches)
• Dasarajna, Parushni, Vasistha, Vishvamitra, Sudasa, Bharatas.

FAQ (one-liners)
Q: What is the Battle of Ten Kings?
A: A Rigvedic account (7.18) of Sudās (Bharatas) defeating a ten-tribe coalition near the Paruṣṇī/Ravi.

Q: Which hymns mention it?
A: Rigveda 7.18 (main), with related material in 7.33 and 7.83.

Q: What is the Bharata Vedic tribe?
A: Sudās’s Vedic tribe; later linked to “Bhārata” (India) and to the Kuru emergence via union with the Purus.

Q: Is Paruṣṇī the same as Ravi?
A: Commonly identified with the Ravi in Punjab.

Q: Why is this battle important?
A: It marks an early Vedic power shift that set the stage for later Kuru developments.
